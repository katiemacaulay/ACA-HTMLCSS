Changing Careers to Web Development

Over the years, I have found that many of my closest friends have delved into the world of web development. In many ways, listening to them talk about what they do, has encouraged me to go and do it also. Here are some of the reasons that I have decided to make a change in careers:

  1. Developers seem to have endless possibilities of learning and opportunities for creative problem-solving.
  2. In the web developing community, there is an openness of sharing knowledge.
  3. Working on projects and collaborating with others is rewarding.
  4. Developers are paid well.

How Being a Teacher May Help Me in the Web Development World

I can't help from thinking about all the incredible experiences that I have had as a music teacher, and how much of these skills could translate into being a developer:

  1. The ability to communicate effectively and percisely when describing my own thought process, and willingness to learn other people's thought processes.
  2. Being able to organize and create systems for projects on time frames.
  3. "Keep it Simple, Silly" is a common phrase I use in my classroom when teaching students about creating compositions. While complex music is really neat, if no one can read it; there's no point.
  4. Believing in the big picture and commiting to the process, even if that means having to work a little bit more than planned.
  5. Staying positive by looking for ways to grow and learn.
  6. The ability to improvise solutions for problems on the spot.

I have taught English, Math, and Music in many different school settings. Currently I teach Prek-8th Grade students music at a private school in Austin.
